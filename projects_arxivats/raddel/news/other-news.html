NanoteC12 - Carbon nanoscience and nanotechnology August 29th - September 1st (University of Sussex, Brighton, UK)

Created on 16 May 2012

NanoteC is one of the longest running series of international nanoscale carbon conferences in Europe (since 1998). It brings together scientists working with nanoscale carbon materials: nanotubes, graphene, diamond- and fullerene-related nanostructures. While each of these materials attracts its own dedicated community of researchers, NanoteC draws on common themes and allows researchers to share insight into this unique element at the nanoscale.

NanoteC12 will be held at the University of Sussex. For more information, please visit the conference website:

http://www.britishcarbon.org/nanotec/index.html

 

Thirteenth International Conference on the Science and Application of Nanotubes (24-29 June 2012) Brisbane (Australia)

Created on 16 May 2012

The NT conferences are the most influential meetings focusing on nanotube research and have been held for 12 years around the world. The thirteenth one will take place in 2012 in Brisbane, Australia. NT12 will continue the NT tradition of presenting the most exciting and current results in the research, development and applications of nanotubes and related materials. It will present a comprehensive program in carbon and non-carbon (eg BN, TiO2, MoS2) nanotubes and graphene in recognition of the burgeoning interest in these inter-related materials. NT12 will provide an outstanding venue to present and discuss the current state progress, directions and opportunities in a dynamic scientific and social environment.

More information can be found at the conference website:

http://www.nt12.org/
